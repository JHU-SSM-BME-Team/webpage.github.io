Assistive Scribe

Problem: During the scribing process, the scribe may have difficultly catching up to the interaction between the doctor and the patient due to a hinderance in typing speed. Moreover, there may be some information spoken between the patient and the doctor that may be missed.

Solution: An assistive scribe would listen to the patient doctor interaction and suggest information for the scribe to fill out in the scribing form. This reduces the workload of the scribe as information is auto-generated. The scribe also has the option to accept or reject suggestions.

The information that can be suggested goes as follows:

  • Symptoms of what the patient is experiencing, where the patient is experiencing those symptoms, for how long, the severity, etc.
  • General information about the patient, including their name, age, occupation, etc.
  • Family history
  • Medication, to be prescribed, or currently prescribed, the dosage amount, the frequency, etc.
  • Additional capabilities can be found in the AWS Medical Comprehend section.

Documentation Assistant

Problem: Nurses and other personnel currently spend time to fill out information about a patient from forms. These forms also come in a variety of different formats, making it harder for personnel to recognize the correct information to populate fields with information about the patient.

Solution: Rather than having personnel populate information about a patient, a computer can automatically recognize key information from forms despite these forms being in a variety of different formats.

This tool can also be helpful for billing purposes, where a computer can automatically detect information from medical bills and populate them to an existing system.

ICD-10-CM Billing Helper

Problem: Workers often have to look up ICD-10-CM codes for a patient visit and populate them in a billing form for each patient. For instance, a doctor’s notes after a patient visit with diagnoses listed requires a human to lookup that diagnoses in the ICD-10-CM codes. Our team proposes that it can be done automatically.

Solution: A computer can look through a doctor’s patient notes and identify the ICD-10-CM codes associated with symptoms or diseases specified in the note. The computer can then populate this information for each patient automatically. This information can also be populated into their corresponding medical bill.

Other information that is required for billing can also be retrieved such as the patient information, past history, etc.

AWS Medical Comprehend

AWS Medical Comprehend can identify information from a given body of text. The information that can be retrieved goes as follows:

RxNorm Concepts

  • The name of a medication
  • The dosage of the medication
  • The frequency of taking that medication
  • How the medication is to be taken

ICD-10-CM Concepts

  • Disease names and their associated ICD-10-CM concepts,
  • The symptoms of the patient
  • The locations of the symptoms (which body area is the patient experiencing pain).

SNOMED CT Concepts

  • Same as above

Entities

  • Patient information - Age, Occupation, and other information describing the patient.
  • Past history - when the patient experienced certain symptoms and experienced certain diseases.
  • Symptoms that the patient is experiencing
  • The locations of the symptoms (which body area is the patient experiencing pain).
  • The name of a medication
  • The dosage of the medication
  • The frequency of taking that medication
  • How the medication is to be taken

AWS Transcribe
